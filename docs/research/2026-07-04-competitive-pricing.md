# Competitive Pricing Research: Accountant/Bookkeeping-Firm Accounting Software (2025-2026 USD)

*Research completed 2026-07-04. All figures in USD. Prepared to size pricing headroom for a multi-tenant double-entry platform sold to firms on a **base-fee-per-firm + per-client/month + per-module** model.*

> **Methodology & confidence note:** Official Intuit, Xero, and Sage US pricing pages are bot-protected and repeatedly returned 503/403/timeout on automated fetch. Figures marked "confirmed" are cross-corroborated across 3-4 independent secondary sources (NerdWallet, Woodard, Firm of the Future, CPA Practice Advisor, Accounting Today, costbench) rather than a raw first-party HTML pull unless otherwise stated. Directly-fetched pages (FreshBooks, Wave, Zoho Books, Puzzle, Bench, Digits, Pilot) are flagged as such and are highest-confidence. **Verify the Intuit/Xero numbers in a browser before putting them in a published deck.**

## ⚠️ Headline finding — Intuit just validated your exact pricing model

The single most important data point for this analysis surfaced mid-research: **Intuit is discontinuing QuickBooks Online Accountant (QBOA) and replacing it with the "Intuit Accountant Suite,"** announced Feb 5, 2026. The new firm-facing pricing is structured **exactly** as base-fee + per-client + per-module:

- **Free "Core" tier** (base platform access stays free)
- **"Accelerate" plan: $149/firm/month** ← a flat firm-level base fee
- **"Books Close" add-on: $8/client/month** (≤50 clients) or **$6/client/month** (>50 clients) ← per-client, per-module, with volume tiering
- Timeline: paid tiers effective ~May 1, 2026 (one source says July 1 — unresolved date conflict); QBOA fully retired Dec 2026; ProAdvisor Program replaced Jan 2027 by a new (unpriced) global partner program.

*Source: [CPA Practice Advisor, Feb 9 2026](https://www.cpapracticeadvisor.com/2026/02/09/intuit-is-discontinuing-quickbooks-online-accountant-and-replacing-it-with-intuit-accountant-suite/177698/); [Accounting Today](https://www.accountingtoday.com/news/intuit-replacing-quickbooks-online-accountant-with-intuit-accountant-suite). Confirmed 2026, very recent.*

**Implication:** the market leader is moving *toward* precisely the three-part model you're pricing for. That both de-risks the model and sets concrete anchors ($149 firm base, $6-8/client per module).

## 4. Base/platform-fee practices

- **The dominant pattern is $0 base fee** — QBOA, Xero HQ, Sage Accountant Edition, Zoho, FreshBooks all give firms free portal/practice access and monetize purely through per-client subscriptions. "Free to join, monetize via client seats" is the industry norm.
- **The exception — and the new direction — is Intuit's Accountant Suite: a flat $149/firm/month "Accelerate" tier** on top of a free Core tier, plus per-client module fees. This is the first mainstream flat firm-level base fee in the segment, effective 2026.
- **Bill.com charges a $49/mo accountant console fee** — a small firm-level base fee, the closest existing analog to a platform fee.
- Takeaway: **a firm-level base fee is uncommon but no longer unprecedented** — the market leader is introducing exactly this, which gives cover to charge one.

## 6. Synthesis — realistic pricing headroom for base + per-client + per-module

**1. Your three-part model is now the market-validated structure.** Intuit's Accountant Suite ($149 firm base + $6-8/client module) is a near-exact template. You are not inventing an unfamiliar pricing shape — you're matching where the leader is going. That's a de-risking signal.

**2. Per-client/month headroom is wide and the market is opaque — that's the opportunity.** Public per-client firm prices span **$3 (Xero Ledger write-up) to $250 (Digits Pro)**, but the striking finding is that **almost every firm program hides its per-client price behind "contact sales"** (Pilot, Puzzle, Zoho, FreshBooks, Digits' new outcome model). Only Digits publishes a real grid. **Transparent per-client pricing is itself a differentiator.** Realistic bands to anchor against:
   - **Budget/write-up tier: $3-10/client** (Xero Ledger/Cashbook, Intuit Books Close module) — for firms doing compliance-only books.
   - **Full-ledger tier: $20-55/client** (QBO wholesale Simple Start/Essentials $27-53; Xero wholesale $20-52) — the volume sweet spot.
   - **Premium/advanced tier: $80-250/client** (QBO Plus/Advanced wholesale $81-193; Digits $250) — multi-entity, AI, or high-complexity clients.
   A **$15-40/client/month** core price sits comfortably inside where QBO and Xero wholesale land, undercutting them modestly while leaving room to go up-market.

**3. A firm-level base fee is defensible but must be justified.** The norm is $0, so a base fee is friction — *unless* you frame it like Intuit ($149 for a value tier) or Bill.com ($49 console). Headroom exists for roughly a **$0 entry / $49-149 firm base** for a premium tier that bundles practice-management, unlimited seats, and consolidated billing. Keep a **free or low base tier** to match the industry's free-portal expectation and avoid pricing out small firms.

**4. Modules should be bundled-into-tiers, not nickel-and-dimed — except payroll and AP.** No competitor sells AI or fixed-assets as standalone SKUs; they bundle into higher tiers. The two things that *are* reliably sold as separate metered modules are **payroll** (base $49-50 + $6-6.50/employee is the market rate) and **AP automation** (~$45-89/user or per-transaction). Model your per-module pricing on those: **payroll ~$40-50 base + $6/employee**, **AP/AR/expenses as $5-15/client/month module add-ons** (matching Intuit's $6-8 Books Close anchor).

**5. Net headroom summary.** A structure like **$0 free tier / optional $49-99 firm base for premium + $15-40/client-month core + $6-12/client-month per module (payroll priced separately at ~$45 + $6/employee)** sits squarely inside competitive wholesale economics, undercuts QBO/Xero retail, matches Intuit's new firm-suite shape, and exploits the market's biggest weakness — **nobody publishes transparent per-client firm pricing.** Transparency plus the multi-tenant/multi-entity architecture is the wedge.
